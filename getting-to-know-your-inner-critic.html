Getting to Know Your Inner Critic

Get curious, not combative

Most of us either ignore our Inner Critic, argue with it, or try to silence it. None of that really works. This exercise takes a different approach: getting to know what it's actually trying to do for you.

Underneath the harsh voice is almost always a part trying to protect you from something. This won't take long, and there's no right way to answer.

Step 1 of 7
Which Critic are you working with today?

If you've taken the Inner Critic quiz, pick whichever one felt strongest. If not, pick the one whose voice you hear most often.

Step 2 of 7
Notice the pattern
When does this Critic show up, and what does it say?
Step 3 of 7
Its positive intent
What is it trying to accomplish by judging or pushing you?

Even harsh voices usually believe they're helping. Ask it directly: what are you trying to do for me?

Step 4 of 7
Its fear
What is it afraid would happen if it stopped?

Critics rarely let go easily. That's usually because they believe something bad will happen without them.

Step 5 of 7
What it's protecting you from
What pain, rejection, or shame is it trying to shield you from?
Step 6 of 7
Your response
Write something back to it, acknowledging what it's been trying to do.

You don't have to agree with how it talks to you. You can appreciate the effort while asking for something different.
