What you're carrying

Inside every one of us is a place where unprocessed emotion goes. Not the feelings you cried through, talked through, or genuinely let go of. The ones you buried. The ones you swallowed. The ones you convinced yourself didn't matter.

I call that place your anger tank.

Throughout your life, every time something hurt you and you didn't process it, that emotion found its way into your anger tank. Every time someone let you down, crossed a boundary, betrayed your trust, or made you feel small - and you pushed it aside instead of dealing with it - another drop was added.

On its own, each drop may not seem significant. But over time, they accumulate until even the smallest irritation is enough to make the tank overflow.

The tank doesn't empty on its own. It simply keeps filling until there's no more room.

That's why something seemingly small can trigger such a big reaction. It isn't really about that one moment. It's about everything that's already sitting beneath it.

Most people don't realise how full their tank is. In fact, many people don't even realise they're carrying one - until they start naming what's inside it.

Why this matters

A full anger tank leaves you constantly carrying emotional weight. It keeps your nervous system on edge, makes you more reactive than you'd like to be, and starts affecting situations that were never really about the original hurt.

The anger itself isn't the problem. Anger is information. The problem is what happens when anger is never processed, never expressed, and never released. It stays trapped inside, building pressure until something small finally causes the tank to overflow.

This tool isn't about suppressing your anger or pretending you're fine. It's about safely releasing what you've been carrying so it no longer controls your reactions today.

How it works
  1. 1
    Fill your tank. Add every anger event you can remember - it doesn't matter whether it happened two weeks or forty years ago. Once you get started, you'll probably remember more than you expected. One memory leads to another. Let them come.
  2. 2
    Choose one. Tap the event that still holds the most emotional charge for you. The tool will guide you through what happened and everything you've been carrying. Say everything. Cry. Swear. Shout. No rules. No judgement.
  3. 3
    Your letter. The tool shapes everything you've shared into a letter addressed directly to that person. Your words. Your rawness. Nothing softened. This letter is never meant to be sent - its purpose is to help you express everything you've been holding inside.
  4. 4
    Burn it. Read it back. Then burn it. The burning is the completion - it's the moment you choose not to carry this particular weight any longer. Keeping the letter only gives you the opportunity to return to it and reconnect with the anger you just worked so hard to release.
