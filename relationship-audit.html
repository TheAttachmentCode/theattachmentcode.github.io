The Relationship Audit

Recognise Your Patterns. Rewrite Your Story.

This tool doesn't tell you what's wrong with your relationship. It shows you the dynamic you're living inside — and where you have real power to change it.

Before You Begin

Before you answer anything, ask yourself — am I describing what actually happened, or what I felt about what happened? Both matter, but they are different things. This tool only works if you describe the behaviour, not the story you've built around it.

There are no right answers here. Only honest ones. You don't need to know your attachment style, or theirs, to use this tool. You just need to be willing to look clearly at what keeps happening — and at your own part in it.

The Pattern

These questions are about them. Describe what actually happens — not your interpretation of why. The more specific and honest you are, the more accurate your audit will be. Speak or type your answers.

Question 01 of 05

What tends to trigger them — either something to do with you or the relationship, or something going on outside of it?

Question 02 of 05

When they're triggered, what do they actually do — both in the moment, and afterwards once things have settled?

Question 03 of 05

How do they typically handle conflict?

Question 04 of 05

When uncertainty rises between you, what do you sense is going on for them internally?

Question 05 of 05

When things feel uncertain, what do you actually see them do?

Your Role

Now the lens turns to you. You can't change them. You can change what you do next. Describe your own responses as honestly as you described theirs.

Question 06 of 12

When they pull away or react, what do you typically do?

Question 07 of 12

How do you handle conflict?

Question 08 of 12

When this pattern is in motion — however it shows up for you — what happens in your body and mind?

Question 09 of 12

What have you tried that hasn't worked?

Question 10 of 12

What do you find yourself doing to keep the peace?

Question 11 of 12

When this pattern plays out, how does it typically resolve or settle?

Question 12 of 12

What specific behaviours — from either of you — keep repeating, no matter what changes?

Integration

These questions bring the pattern and your role together. There are no right answers here. Only honest ones.

Integration 01

Where does this relationship stand right now? Are you together and in regular contact? On a break? Has something specific just happened? Give as much detail as you can — this shapes everything that follows.

Integration 02

After an argument or a period of distance, who usually reaches out first? What tends to happen after that?

Integration 03

When things are good between you, what are they like? What do you value most about your partner, and about this relationship? What keeps drawing you back?

Integration 04

Underneath the specific incidents, what's the recurring story, fear, or accusation that keeps resurfacing between you?

Integration 05

Have you seen this pattern before — either earlier in this relationship, or in relationships before this one? When did you first notice it?

Integration 06

What have you stopped doing or stopped saying — because you learned it wasn't safe or worth the cost?

Integration 07

Does a story run through your mind when conflict or distance happens — and if so, how different does it look once you're calm? If that's not really how it shows up for you, that's worth noting too.

Integration 08

What does staying in this dynamic cost you — in your energy, your sense of self, your life?

Integration 09

What does staying in this dynamic give you? What would you lose if it ended?

Integration 10

If your relationship was exactly the same as it is right now in five years' time — what would that mean for you?

Integration 11 — Honesty Mirror

Is there anything in how you've answered today that might reflect how you feel about the situation rather than what actually happened? Be honest with yourself here — this is just for you.

Integration 12

What outcome do you actually want — for this relationship, and for yourself? And what would need to change to get there — in you, in them, or both?

Integration 13

Did you take time to heal between your last significant relationship and this one? Do you think anything unresolved came with you into this one?

Integration 14

Have you ever sought support for these patterns — therapy, coaching, inner work? If not, what's stopped you?
